## 🛠️ Getting started with your first project

Open the application from your desktop shortcut or your start menu. You will see a dashboard that lists your projects. 

Click the "New Project" button to start. Give your project a name and choose a folder on your computer to save your files. The app creates the necessary structure for you automatically. 

The sidebar on the left shows your project files. Click any file to open it in the editor. You can edit text, layout, and style from this central view. Use the "Preview" button at the top of the window to see your changes in real-time. This preview updates every time you save your work. 

The application uses standard web file types. You can open these files in any web browser if you wish to view your work outside the editor.

Check your firewall settings if you cannot preview your project. The application needs local access to show your site in the browser. Allow "topcoat" through your security settings if the app asks for network permissions.

Reach out to the support team if specific errors persist. Provide the version number found in the "About" menu to help them diagnose the issue. 

## 📋 Best practices for your workflow

Organize your project files early. Use clear names for your folders and images. This saves effort as your project grows larger. 

Back up your files frequently. Copy your project folder to a cloud storage service or an external drive. This protects your work against hardware failures. 

Test your pages in different browsers. While the built-in preview works well, checking your work in Chrome, Edge, and Firefox ensures a consistent experience for your final users.
